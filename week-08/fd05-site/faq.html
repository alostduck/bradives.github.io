Frequently Asked Questions

Questions we are asked often

Have a question for us? We are always happy to answer question's you may have for us as best as we can. Please feel free to call us at (360) 793-1179. This list is simply for quick reference.

  • Is there a burn ban on?
    You can always find up to date county wide burn ban information at
    The Snohomish county outdoor burning information page of the Snohomish county
    fire marshals office.     We do not issue burn permits in the district and generally outdoor burning is not allowed without a permit.
  • Can I get my blood pressure checked at the Station?
    Yes! feel free to come during normal business hours to have your blood pressure checked by any one of our firefighter/EMTs. There is no need to call ahead or set an appointment.
  • Why does the Station appear un-staffed at night?
    It is staffed, however our night shift is in the building behind the station since our current station does not have sleeping quarters. Crews are still alerted to calls. However since we are not inside the station at night, if you are coming to us, you will need to call 9-1-1 and tell them where you are. The best practice is to go to the front door of the station and wait for us there.
  • Can I come stop by the station for a tour?
    Of course!! We are always willing to give a station tour! Please note that if you are planning on bringing a large group or are wanting us to host a birthday party, please contact our admin to set up your event!
  • Can I listen to your radio?
    Our radio is not available to the public to use or listen to directly. However there are websites that play live radio scanners, like Broadcastify (this is to the north feed that we can be heard on)
