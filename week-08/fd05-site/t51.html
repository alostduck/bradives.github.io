About Tender 51

Tender 51 is a 2002 Kenworth with a 3000 gallon water tank on the back. This unit is primarily used for water supply in areas where there are no hydrants or access to them is limited. The Tender carries a crew of two. The unit is capable of fighting fire as well and is set up with two basic attack hose loads and a top mounted deck gun water cannon that can be detached and used as a ground water cannon. The unit also carries a portable, foldable water tank and can dump it's entire supply of 3000 gallons directly into the foldable tank via a rear chute. The idea is that the engine is supplied from the fold-a-tank (as it is called) while the Tender runs to the nearest water source, refills and dumps again. This is important as firefighting operations can use hundrends of gallons per minuet.

Tools carried on T-51 include:

  • fold-a-tank, hard suction hose and equipment used for drawing water from just about any source, including rivers and streams.
  • general firefighting hand tools and SCBAs
  • medical kit
  • Portable small suction pump. This smaller pump can be used for forest fire operations and can supply and power a hose without the tender needing to be presant.
