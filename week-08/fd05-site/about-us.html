History and Leadership

History of the Department

The department was founded in 1911 and was an all volunteer department until the late 1990s when the department recognized the need for full time staffing and hired it's first two career members. In the early 2000's the department hired several more full time firefighters from within the volunteer ranks. The most recent full time firefighter being hired in 2019, we now employ 3 full time firefighters, 20-30 part-time and volunteer firefighters, 2 full time officers, 1 full time Deputy Chief, 1 part time Deputy Chief and 1 full time Chief.

The station was originally located right off U.S. highway 2 at 5th street in what is today the city's post office. In 1973, The current station was built at 304 Alder ave. At the time, volunteers simply responded from home when alerted to a call and came to the station for drills. As the city grew, the department realized the need for around the clock staffing, beginning to use volunteer and then part time staff at night, an initially staffing of 2 firefighter/EMTs, some years later 3 firefighter/EMTs and since 2016 staffing has been 4-5 part-time/volunteer firefighter/EMTs at night and 2-3 Full Time firefighter/EMTs and 1-3 part time/volunteer firefighter/EMTs during the day. crews work 12 hour shifts or more. Staff that lives in district can respond from home as needed also.

1938 Howe Engine

As of June 1937, the City of Sultan authorized the purchase of a new fire engine, which would be delivered in 1938. The 1938 Howe Northern Centrifugal pumper which was built on a Chevrolet chassis, came completely equipped with a hose bed, 500 gpm pump, 150 gallon booster tank, two CO-2 fire extinguishers, and 1000 feet of 2.5 diameter inch hose. Something that was unique to this specific engine was the heavy aluminum cab top, which had not been fitted to that model of vehicle before and this vehicle was one of the first in the nation to have it.

Today, the Howe is still kept running by the Sultan volunteer firefighters association, and is used annually in the shindig parade, typically held in the first week of July.

Model T apparatus

Also maintained by The Sultan Firefighters Association is the 1911 Model T, which did not feature a pump but did carry a hose bed. The Model T is also used annually in the Shindig parade.

The lieutenants

The lieutenants over see day time operations and crew training. Occasionally they work overtime at night when night shift is low on staffing and also act as battalion chiefs at night on weekends as needed.
