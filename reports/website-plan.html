Personal website project plan

A replacement website project for fire department with old, antiquated website.

The site proposed is a replacement for a rural fire department currently in serch of a replacement for their antiquated and neglected webiste. The title will be "small town FD's webiste"

The target audience

  • Residents within the fire district
  • prosepctive volunteers and employeees
  • people looking for information on yard waste burning and current burn ban info
  • People looking for information the departments operations and programs offered
    • fire explorers
    • CPR and first aid training
    • youth and adult public fire safety education
    • information about upcoming department events and involvement in community events
  • Fire deparment enthusiasts

The site purpose

The site's purpose will be a propsed replacement or upgrade from a rural deparment's current neglected and out of date website. The main purpose of the fire deparment's website is to provide information about current events and operations as well as education for the residents the department serves and the general public.

Topics covered on the site will include fire safety information, information about department units and equipment, programs and training offered as well as any news the department has released about events that are ongoing.

Comparable sites

These three sites all contain parts of what I believe the site needs to have:

  • Snohomish Fire District #4

    • Snohomish (whom i also work for) has recently redone their webpage. it has several things I like:

    • the background features the people working there and gives the firefighters and medics a good image
    • status of burn bans is right on the home page, easy information to find
    • nav is a bit long but does have commonly searched for information

  • Lutherville Volunteer Fire Company

    • Found this from a simple google search. The front page is the exact kind of page that nerdy firefighters look for. You might have never considered firefighting in your life and the front page makes you want to work there. In fact, there is a recruitment element on the front page that is very prominent. other things I like:

    • page is clearly geared towords perspective recruits and the public they work for
    • current events feed with recent news
    • each of their vehicles has it's own page. nice.

  • LAFD home page

    • A lot of big city fire departments have webpages but LAFD seems to have a super detailed one covering literally everything they do, emergency response related or not. There's even a page about their movie set unit! (Imagine that job.) Other cool stuff:

    • the whole site is super public information oriented. Any and literally all questions you may have about the LAFD have answers here. Even the FAQ has a link for "I'm not sure who to contact".
    • Like Lutherville, the front page features a currrent events feed but also a live feed to their social media, which is a big thing these days as well.

Main content

Stuff I want to include on the site:

  • A recrtuing ad. The department is rural and relies heavily on volunteers and part time staff and because of this departemnts experince and training, the part-time and volunteer staff are constantly being hired away to full time jobs in larger departments. This might need to be included in the recruitment.
  • Current events and news, a feed of some kind. The city has a facebook page where citizens are constatly asking "what are these sirens about?" and "where can i get info about the Fire station hosting a birthday party?" This could be their one stop shop.
  • Current events and news, perhaps live social media windows or views (twitter or facebook)
  • Nav. Want to keep this simple yet informatative. Home, about, public education, CPR/First aid calsses and perhaps a sign-up. Contact info at least.
  • Pictures of memebers in action, pictures of the vehicles. Familiarity is a comfort thing, citizens and also Firefighting buffs (see: nerds) can also enjoy looking at and learning about the vehicles and equipment
  • perhaps a live scanner to listen to radio traffic? (unsure of the legal side of this though.)

Personality

The personality of this site will be inviting, assittive and have a humble yet prideful feel to it. The idea is that the visitor feels welcome and that prosepctive employees want to work there by viewing the site.

Limitations

Being located in a rural area, some parts of the population most likely expereince lower speeds in their internet connection. The site might be constructed for a mobile first design for less data being sent, hopefully leading to faster loading.
